Stand Up Paddle expedition along the Amazon River

CONTENT, PHOTOGRAPHY, VIDEO, PRINT

An expedition led by Doug Stoup, one of the leading polar explorers alive today. This trip embarks on the exploration of the Amazon River and many of its tributaries, discovering its various cultures, flora and fauna. Home to nearly a third of all of the world’s species and many diverse and beautiful cultures, this expedition is a yearly favourite for Stonesthrow to collaborate with Ice Axe on.

We were hired by Doug and the Ice Axe team to document this once in a lifetime adventure. The content captured not only for marketing purposes however much of it being used for scientific and educational purposes.

The Amazon Rainforest, one of the most important ecosystems on earth. As the largest tropical rainforest in the world it plays such an important part in maintaining the planets temperature. Commonly mistaken for the ‘lungs of the Earth’ due to the large amount of oxygen produced, evidence now suggests rainforests don’t have much effect on the world’s oxygen supply due to the decomposition of dead plant matter.

This consumes around the same amount of oxygen as the living plants produce. However these forests still plays a major role in the global ecosystem. They are now referred to as the ‘air conditioners of the Earth’ due to their dark shades absorbing heat from the sun, ultimately playing a key role in regulating global temperature.
